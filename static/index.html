How it works

This course at a glance

1 Your course

Give your course a name (used to tag every import and export), or grab its real title and weekly outline straight from a Moodle export.

🎓 Course name

This name flows everywhere automatically — transcripts, flashcards, NotebookLM and Notion exports all get tagged with it. You can change it any time from the bar at the top.

🏫 Grab course info from Moodle

Point at a saved Moodle course folder (or its course/view_php.html) to pull the course title and week/topic outline — then use it as your course name in one click. Works with any Moodle course.

2 Import your stuff

Keep adding whatever you have for this course. Everything lands in your Library, ready to export. Pick a source below.

📦 Export everything for AI all sources

One click brings everything you've imported together — lecture transcripts, converted documents and Notion pages — into clean Markdown under _notebooklm/, plus a single combined everything_pack.md you can drop into NotebookLM or hand to any other AI.

Or export one kind at a time

📓 NotebookLM sources

Clean, de-timestamped Markdown (one file per lecture) under _notebooklm/ — ready to upload to NotebookLM as sources.

🗃️ Notion study database (CSV)

One row per lecture — Name, Week, Date, Topic, Tags, Status, Summary. In Notion: Import → CSV, then set Tags to Multi-select.

🎬 Subtitles & extra formats

Generate captions and other formats from your transcripts — saved next to each lecture and shown in your Library.

🃏 Anki flashcards Anki-ready

Pulls definitions and acronyms out of your transcripts and auto-tags them by course · week · topic. Exports an Anki-importable .txt and a .csv under _flashcards/. Summaries make the cleanest cards.

🏷️ Categorize an existing deck (already have flashcards? tag them)

Paste a deck (CSV or tab-separated: front, back[, tags]) — or give a file path — and it adds course/week/topic tags by matching your lectures' vocabulary.
